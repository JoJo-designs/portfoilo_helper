Monster Cards

I completed this project after I had graduated college. The goal was to create a set of flash cards about a topic that would be interesting. I like spooky stuff and I create a set of card on folktale monsters from around the world. Each card needed an illustration and information that related.

The cards in black and white with shades of gray. The illustrations were drawn by myself and are made up of two components. The illustration of the monsters which are unique to each card. The second part a frame which would be a representation of the environment the monster would be found in. The cave frame for cave dwelling monsters ect. These frames are not unique to each card and I reused the same three frames. Doing this saved time and created a layed effect in the images. The frames all have a tattered ribbon on the bottom the display the monsters name in a black letter font

The back of the cards are simple an clean. The monsters name is displayed at the top of the page and under that is the palace the legend originated from or believe to have originated from. There is also a short paragraph about the monster these range from descriptions to stories and real world explanations that may explain where the legend began.

This is one of the cards. The monster depicted is the Grootslang. Believed to be a combination of a snake and elephant. The creature is said to dwell in a cave in south africa that locals claim is full of diamonds. The card uses the cave frame.
I draw the illustration with paper and ink. Each drawing is 4 inchs by two inchs. After I scanned them into the computer and vectorized them. which I did so I could remove the background easily. After I could layer them under the frame nicely.
This 3D rendering of the box shows what the box would look like. unlike most card boxes that open at the top this one is a two piece design with a top and bottom half.
